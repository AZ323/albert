Dear Data: Everyday Code

Code is in every aspect of our lives, it can be massively impactful like increasing transportation and business affairs, or it can be in smaller things like social media and Alexa. Either way, code is used every day and without it, our societies would fundamentally change as a whole. The dear data visualization activity put into perspective the amount of time I was truly spending on social media. It was surprising because day to day you don't notice how much time is taken up by unimportant phone usage. The activity did make me consciously decide to use my phone less for the following week, and a noticeable difference in the time I have left in a day is quite surprising. The one thing that surprised me about the data was the ratio of picking up my phone and going into an app compared to how much time I truly spent in the app. While TikTok was the leading app in hours, it was the least first picked after first opening up my phone, while Snapchat was the most by a landslide, beating other apps by three or even four times.

Code and digital technology impact on education

Learning in a classroom with just paper and pen for me and many of my peers was a challenging, strenuous, and time-wasting practice that has, thankfully, completely gone out of fashion in college. Code and digital technology change everything we know about how we learn and do tasks and improve it exponentially. From the simple things of not having to use different writing utensils and extra pages to the complexity of being able to manipulate PDFs in meticulous ways, it isn't surprising that in all my lectures everyone has a computer or tablet on their desks. By learning more about how we can use code and technology in classrooms we will be able to move past our outdated teaching and learning styles and improve our society as a whole.
